Realistic Research: University, Web-based, and Local Resources

This lesson is for students starting research in the TCID, IA, or Ph.D. Technical Communication program. It introduces essential strategies for finding and requesting academic resources.

Getting Started

Access library services through myIIT using your Hawk Card. Use the proxy connection for full access to library resources.

Research Timelines

Think realistically: time to get, read, and use resources must be shorter than the time left until your deadline.

Being Realistic: Don’t try to read a whole book in one night. Plan accordingly.

Requesting Resources

You can request articles and books from other libraries using MyILL and WorldCat. Be sure the timing works for your schedule.

Local Libraries

You can use I-Share libraries and public libraries. Check their policies and hours before visiting.

Being Realistic: Always call or check online before going to a library to make sure it’s open and accessible.

Finding Resources on the Web

Use tools like Google Scholar, Google Books, and Web of Science to find open or referenced materials online.

Tip: Special issues or edited collections can help you understand the bigger picture of your topic.
